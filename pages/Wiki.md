- ## Research
	- [[wiki/research/phd-thesis]] — Gabriel Béna's PhD thesis — "Emergence, Modularity and Self-Organisation in Neural Systems" (Imperial College London, submitted ~Feb 2026). Unifying document spanning four papers across two themes: modularity (Ch1+Ch2) and self-organisation (Ch3+Ch4). Contains the definitive literature review, the 2×2 PP/UP/PS/US framework, and the grand vision of a self-organising neuromorphic brain. ( #thesis #modularity )
	- [[wiki/research/dynamics-specialization]] — Structural modularity does not guarantee functional specialization; specialization emerges reliably only under resource constraints (few neurons, sparse inter-module synapses) + separable environment. Specialization is dynamic, not static. ( #modularity #functional-specialization )
- ## References — most recent 5
	- [[wiki/refs/fernando-2017-pathnet]] — Agents are *pathways* through a giant network that discover which modules to reuse per task; a task's path is frozen and later paths re-evolve around it → transfer without catastrophic forgetting. The closest prior art to growing-computation's recruit/freeze/reuse. ( #continual-learning #modularity )
	- [[wiki/refs/whitacre-2010-degeneracy-evolvability]] — Degeneracy — partial functional overlap among structurally different multi-functional components — argued as a central source of biological robustness, multi-scale complexity, and evolvability. ( #degeneracy #robustness )
	- [[wiki/refs/wagner-2005-robustness-evolvability]] — Wagner's synthesis: robustness = function persisting under perturbation; most biological problems have vast neutral spaces; distributed robustness (not redundancy) dominates; robustness ↔ evolvability; non-living robustness is self-organised. ( #robustness #evolvability )
	- [[wiki/refs/wagner-2005-ch20-man-made-systems]] — The same robustness principles — distributed robustness and evolved robustness — appear in engineered systems; shown on telecom networks and on field-programmable transistor arrays whose fault-tolerance evolves under fault pressure. ( #robustness #evolvable-hardware )
	- [[wiki/refs/wagner-2005-ch19-self-organization]] — Robustness in non-living systems arises from self-organisation, not natural selection — perturbations themselves drive a system toward robust states; "fragile systems are fleeting." ( #robustness #self-organisation )
