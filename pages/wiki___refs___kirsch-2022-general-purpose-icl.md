- **General-Purpose In-Context Learning by Meta-Learning Transformers (GPICL)** — Kirsch, Harrison, Sohl-Dickstein & Metz (2022), arXiv:2212.04458 (Google Research Brain Team + IDSIA). Full PDF read 2026-06-08.
	- The paper that **characterises *when* a meta-learner stops memorising and starts to generalise** — Gabriel's anchor for how an [[NCA]] optimiser ([[SODC]]) might become *task/topology agnostic*. Companion to [[wiki/refs/kirsch-2021-meta-learning-backprop]].

- ## Abstract (extracted)
	- Modern machine learning requires system designers to specify aspects of the learning pipeline… One particularly ambitious goal of meta-learning is to train general-purpose in-context learning algorithms from scratch, using only black-box models with minimal inductive bias… In this paper we show that Transformers and other black-box models can be meta-trained to act as general-purpose in-context learners. We characterize transitions between algorithms that generalize, algorithms that memorize, and algorithms that fail to meta-train at all, induced by changes in model size, number of tasks, and meta-optimization. We further show that the capabilities of meta-trained algorithms are bottlenecked by the accessible state size (memory) determining the next prediction, unlike standard models which are thought to be bottlenecked by parameter count…

- ## The setup
	- A **vanilla Transformer** (minimal inductive bias) is fed a sequence of `(x_i, y_{i-1})` token pairs and predicts the query label `y'` for `x'` **purely in its forward pass** — no gradient steps at meta-test (this is what makes it *in-context*, not gradient-based like MAML).
	- **Tasks are generated by augmenting one base dataset**: random linear input projections `A ~ N(0, 1/Nx)` + random output-label permutations `ρ`. This manufactures a large, diverse task distribution from MNIST/Fashion/CIFAR-10 (random projection strips spatial structure, which isn't central to the task). It lets them dial *task diversity* as a knob (Algorithm 1).

- ## The transition (the centrepiece)
	- Two orthogonal axes (Table 1): does the algorithm **learn** (improve with more in-context examples) and does it **generalise** (to unseen tasks/datasets)? Four regimes: **task memorisation** (neither) → **task identification** (learns, but only on seen-like tasks) → **zero-shot generalisation** (generalises, doesn't learn) → **general-purpose learning algorithm** (both).
	- **Insight 3 — algorithmic transitions (Fig 4).** Increasing the **number of meta-training tasks** drives a transition **memorise → identify → general learning-to-learn**. The general regime needed **≥ 2¹³ ≈ 8192 tasks** (Insight 1). The same transition also appears along **model size** (Fig 2): the model switches from a memorisation solution to a *learning* solution as it grows — and only the *sequence* model generalises to unseen tasks (an MLP never does, Fig 2a/c).
	- **Insight 2 — the data distribution controls generality.** Simple augmentations suffice; you tune generalisation by tuning the task distribution, not the architecture.

- ## State/memory is the bottleneck, not parameters (Insight 4)
	- **§4.2 / Fig 5:** across architectures, the **size of the accessible state (memory) at meta-test time most strongly predicts** general-learning capability — *parameter count correlates far less well*. Transformers win here because self-attention gives a large state `N_S = O(N_K N_L N_T)` (keys×layers×sequence-length). The learned algorithm has to *store learning progress in its state*, so state is the true capacity for learning-to-learn. (A useful reframing of "what is the capacity of an in-context learner.")

- ## Meta-optimisation is hard — loss plateaus (§4.3)
	- Meta-training across many tasks produces an extended **loss plateau** where the model first memorises tiny biases, then **drops sharply** into the learning-to-learn solution (Fig 6, ~35k steps). *"Being able to first memorise slightly enables the following learning-to-learn phase"* — the authors connect this to **grokking** (Power et al. 2022). ^[wiki-gap: grokking has no wiki page]
	- Whether it memorises / generalises / stays stuck depends on (#tasks × batch size) (Fig 7). Interventions: **bigger meta-batch** (lower gradient variance), **a different meta-optimiser** (small Adam ε / sign-based updates), and **biasing the data distribution as a *curriculum*** (memorise-first as a stepping stone to learn-to-learn).

- ## VSML contrast + results
	- **Two routes to one general learning algorithm.** Table 2 compares GPICL (*in-context, black-box, minimal bias*) with [[wiki/refs/kirsch-2021-meta-learning-backprop]] (*in-context, parameter-sharing*), MAML and SGD across MNIST/Fashion/KMNIST/Random/CIFAR-10/SVHN. GPICL *"comes surprisingly close to VSML without requiring the associated inductive bias"* — i.e. **GPICL buys generality with task diversity where VSML buys it with weight-sharing.** Both end at a general LA; the inductive-bias route and the data-diversity route are the two on offer.
	- **§4.4:** GPICL with pre-trained features (ResNet/CLIP) leverages domain knowledge *and* still generalises (~45% CIFAR-10 from 100 examples).

- ## Why it matters here (the user's framing, nuanced)
	- **"When does a meta-learner generalise?" ↔ "when does an NCA optimiser become task/topology agnostic?"** GPICL's answer — *enough task diversity + enough state, past a transition* — is the data-distribution route to a general learning algorithm; [[wiki/refs/kirsch-2021-meta-learning-backprop]]'s weight-sharing is the inductive-bias route. **[[SODC]] plausibly has both**: a *shared rule* (the VSML-style bias) trained on *random topologies/tasks* (the GPICL-style diversity). Its known **scale-freedom — "fixed-topology training overfits to scale; random-topology training enables emergent width scaling"** — is the *same shape* as GPICL's diversity→generalisation transition (training-distribution breadth flips a memorised solution into a general one). A rhyme to test, not a proven identity. ^[claude-synth]
	- **Answers an open question on [[wiki/concepts/in-context-learning]]:** *"which training distribution actually yields a general inner algorithm vs a memorised lookup?"* → task diversity above a threshold, with a memorise→identify→generalise transition; and capacity = *state*, not parameters. ^[inferred]
